SMAC Profile

The SMAC Sisters took over the CARITAS and served the Parish of Toril after arriving in Davao City on January 17, 1998. observing the living conditions of the poor parishioners and families who are unable to treat sick members; the existence of out-of-school children because of financial inability resulting in the majority of them wandering on the streets; very poor living conditions resulting in malnutrition, lacking in hygienic practices and low morale; extreme poverty; and factors that influence for the children to be exposed to physical and sexual abuse.

Observe the poor parishioners' living situations and the families who are unable to take care of sick members. Youth who are unable to attend school due to financial constraints, walking in the streets in large numbers. These people were rounded up by SMAC and given homes. The goal of SMAC is to locate street children who have been left or abandoned, provide them with the chance to better their life through knowledge, and give them a place to call home where they may experience love and care.

Madre Nanda Giamberardini, the institution's mother and founder, founded the group with the intention of offering high-quality assistance to neglected, abandoned, and orphaned children living on the streets. Facilities like providing medical and wellness care, teaching fundamental skills like baking and cooking, and providing spiritual uplift through participation in rosaries and the holy eucharist are all examples of service. The center's founder wanted to raise the quality of service offered to those children by realigning the programs and concretizing its offers and to prepare the center for independence.

The presence of older students, which causes the majority of them to stray in the city due to financial insufficiency; the unfavorable daily environment, which causes hunger, a lack of cleanliness, and poor hygiene; extreme neediness; and other factors that affect the children. The following systems and initiatives are offered to inhabitants, sometimes known as kids. From kindergarten to university, children are sent to school. Financial aid, however, is necessary at the collegiate level.
